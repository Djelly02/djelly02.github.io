About Me

Hello! I’m Jelle Uyttebroek, a mechanical engineering graduate at KU Leuven specializing in robotics and mechatronics. I’m passionate about understanding how complex systems work and finding ways to optimize them. My studies have given me the chance to explore robotics, mechatronics, and system design, and I enjoy tackling challenges where creativity meets technical problem-solving.

When I’m not paining my brain, I like to pain my body. I’m a triathlon enthusiast and this passion has inspired several projects during my studies with the icing on the cake being my thesis together with classified cycling. Like in training, I seek continuous improvements in my work and stay curious towards new things.

I also love traveling, discovering new cultures, and meeting people from different backgrounds. I enjoy experimenting in my free time, whether that’s exploring new training methods, tweaking old bicycles, building creative projects like this portfolio, or simply finding new ways to think about problems.

So who am I? I’m someone who loves learning, building, and sharing ideas. Whether it’s developing innovative solutions, brainstorming over a cup of coffee, or exploring the world, I’m always excited to discover what’s possible next.
