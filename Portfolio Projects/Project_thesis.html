Master thesis: Enhancing the end-of-line test of the Powershift Hub: a multibody dynamic approach

As the final part of my Master in Mechanical Engineering at KU Leuven, I did my thesis in collaboration with Classified Cycling, a manufacturer of advanced bicycle drivetrain components. Together with a colleague, I worked on improving the end-of-line (EOL) test of the Powershift Hub, the company’s flagship two-stage planetary gear system that enables two distinct operating ratios. The objective was to reduce the number of faulty hubs passing the current test.

Project Objective

The Powershift Hub contains shafts, bearings, a planetary gear train, an actuation module, and a clutch mechanism. While the existing EOL test was designed to measure efficiency and functionality, it struggled to reliably detect subtle manufacturing defects. Our task was to enhance the diagnostic capability of the test setup, ensuring earlier and more accurate fault detection.

Approach

We initially explored a data-driven condition monitoring strategy, analyzing large sets of EOL test data using machine learning techniques. However, shortcomings such as poor labeling and uncertainties about whether faults originated during production or later in use limited this approach. Limited collaboration with the company required different approaches.

As a result, the project shifted towards a model-based approach:

  • Developed a multibody dynamic (MBD) model of the Powershift Hub in Simcenter Motion, capturing both operating ratios (direct drive and 0.7 ratio with active gear movement).
  • Simulated different manufacturing faults and evaluated their influence on measurable outputs such as torque, angular velocity, and acceleration.
  • Used the results to guide sensor selection and propose improvements to the EOL test bench design.

Results

  • The MBD simulations identified weaknesses in the current setup and highlighted fault signatures that were invisible to the existing sensors.
  • Demonstrated the benefits of the multibody dynamic model by means of a sensor placement analysis.
  • Delivered recommendations for enhancing the diagnostic power of the EOL test, forming the basis for future integration of condition monitoring in Classified’s production line.

Reflection

This thesis strengthened my expertise in dynamic modeling, vibration analysis, condition monitoring, and machine learning while also providing experience in industry collaboration and problem-solving under data limitations. Despite challenges such as restricted access to real-world sensor data, the virtual simulations provided meaningful insights and actionable recommendations for the company.
